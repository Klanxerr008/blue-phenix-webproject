Ransomware Attack Hits Taiwan-Based Circuit Board Maker Unimicron

Taiwan-based Unimicron Technology (ShenZhen) Corp., a printed circuit board manufacturing giant, suffered a ransomware attack Jan. 30. The company cooperated with an external cyber forensic team to conduct incident analysis and defense as soon as possible and its operations remain limited. The cybercriminals published samples of files it says it exfiltrated from the company’s systems during the attack and threaten to leak everything next week if a ransom is not paid. The Sarcoma gang claimed responsibility for the attack against Unimicron, who has has manufacturing facilities and service centers in Taiwan, China, Germany, and Japan.

Kenya's Business Registration Service Breached, Online Database Down

Kenya's Business Registration Service (BRS) has suffered a major data breach in a cyberattack on that has now left many private companies in the hands of the public. A trove of secret and sensitive data of significant shareholders in registered firms such as National ID, residential address, and phone numbers could be ordered for sale on the dark web due to the breach. A source close to the matter says that BRS executives were holed up in crisis meetings for most of Feb. 1 following the attack, which is thought to have occurred Jan. 31, and carried out by an internal actor. The BRS online database, which charges fees in order to obtain information on Kenyan companies, is down and inaccessible.

Papua New Guinea's Internal Revenue Commission hit in major cyberattack

Papua New Guinea's tax office has been hit with a major cyberattack, pushing systems offline and potentially exposing sensitive data belonging to hundreds of thousands of people and businesses, including Australian companies and citizens. On Jan. 29, the Internal Revenue Commission (IRC) — PNG's version of the Australian Tax Office (ATO) — notified the public of a "system outage" affecting its network. It said a team was working to resolve the issue, however many of the IRC's functions remain offline two weeks later. Sources in Papua New Guinea, an island country in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, and Australia have confirmed the issues were the result of a cyberattack.
